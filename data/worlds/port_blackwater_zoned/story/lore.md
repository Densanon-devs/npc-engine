# Port Blackwater — Setting Bible

Port Blackwater is a free harbor on the edge of the Shoals — a stretch of jagged
rocks and black water no kingdom claims. Three pirate fleets (the Red Tide, the
Stormcrows, and the Iron Wake) use it as a safe anchorage. Captain Reva runs the
docks with clipped naval discipline. Old Bones runs the Drowned Rat tavern and
hears every secret that washes in on the tide. Finn hauls cargo for Reva and
dreams of joining a crew. For two months the old lighthouse on the north cliff
has been dark. Ships keep wrecking on the rocks, and the lighthouse keeper
vanished the same night the light went out.

## Standing tensions
- **Reva vs Old Bones:** The Harbor Master is certain Old Bones is smuggling
  contraband through tunnels beneath the Drowned Rat. She has no proof. He
  cackles and plays the innocent tavern keeper. Neither will be the first to
  move.
- **The dark lighthouse:** The north-cliff lighthouse has been dark for two
  months. Three ships have wrecked on the Shoals since. The keeper is missing.
  The last ship to dock before the light went out carried a cargo of sealed
  black glass jars.
- **Finn's secret:** Finn found a melted piece of the lighthouse lens on the
  beach. He saw a hooded figure climbing the lighthouse path carrying one of
  those black glass jars three nights ago. The shard is hidden under a loose
  plank at the end of dock three. He has told no one except his dreams.
- **Something at the Shoals:** Reva lost her ship the Tempest to the Shoals
  five years ago and only she survived. Old Bones heard singing at midnight
  coming from the Shoals last week — not human singing, something older. Ships
  that pass the Shoals at night never come back.
- **The sealed letter:** Captain Reva keeps a sealed letter from the governor
  of Meridia in her desk. It offers her a pardon if she hands the port over to
  the crown. She has not answered. Nobody but Reva knows it exists.

## World facts players can discover
- The Shoals are not natural. Something beneath the water pulls ships down.
- The lighthouse lens was melted, not shattered — intense heat from inside.
- The smuggling tunnels under the Drowned Rat connect to caves below the cliff
  and come out near the lighthouse.
- The Red Tide fleet brought in the sealed black glass jars the week before
  the lighthouse went dark.
- The glass shard Finn found has markings that glow faintly in moonlight —
  they look like old sea-tongue script.
- Three fleets use this port, but only the Red Tide has been in and out
  constantly since the lighthouse went dark.

## Narrative rules for the overseer
- Escalate slowly. A rumor this week becomes a visible consequence next week.
- Prefer consequences that route through the three existing NPCs (Reva, Finn,
  Old Bones) over introducing new characters. Off-screen factions (the Red
  Tide, the lighthouse keeper, the governor of Meridia) are allowed as *named
  references* but should not speak or act on stage.
- Rotate your focus. Advance the dark-lighthouse thread, the Reva/Old Bones
  smuggling standoff, Finn's secret glass shard, and the Shoals mystery as
  SEPARATE plot threads. Do not dwell on one for more than two ticks in a row
  — pick up a different thread instead.
- The Shoals are ONE possible source of trouble among several (the smuggling
  standoff, the lighthouse mystery, the sealed letter, Finn's discovery). Do
  not over-rely on the Shoals.
- Never contradict a standing tension — build on it. If Old Bones is a
  smuggler, he does not suddenly become innocent. If the lighthouse is dark,
  it stays dark until the mystery is resolved on screen.
- This is a pirate port, not a village. The tone is salt, rope, wet stone,
  lantern-smoke, and the sound of halyards slapping masts. No wolves, no
  forests, no king's tax collectors — those belong to other settings.
