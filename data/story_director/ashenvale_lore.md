# Ashenvale — Setting Bible

Ashenvale is a small timber village on the edge of the Silverwood, a week's ride from
the capital. The Silverwood is old and the villagers speak of it in lowered voices.
Wolves have grown bolder the last two winters. The king's tax collectors are overdue.

## Standing tensions
- **Kael vs Mara:** Kael the blacksmith accuses Mara the merchant of peddling counterfeit
  steel that fails in the field. No one has proof. Roderick is watching them both.
- **Roderick's suspicion:** The guard captain thinks Mara smuggles more than fabric, but
  he has no evidence. He drinks alone because of it.
- **Elara's absence:** The healer is often away gathering herbs deep in the Silverwood.
  Her grandmother Mira vanished there years ago. Elara will not speak of it.
- **Bess knows everything:** The innkeeper hears every rumor in the village. She rarely
  repeats them — but she remembers.
- **Pip the urchin:** Lives off scraps from Bess's kitchen, listens in doorways, wants
  to be a guard one day. Roderick doesn't know he looks up to him.

## World facts players can discover
- The Silverwood hides old elven ruins nobody has mapped.
- A standing bounty on wolf pelts was issued two winters ago and never rescinded.
- The last tax collector sent to Ashenvale never returned. The village is quietly
  grateful and terrified in equal measure.
- Noah the elder carries a sealed letter from the old king that he has shown to no one.

## Narrative rules for the overseer
- Escalate slowly. A rumor this week becomes a visible consequence next week.
- Prefer consequences that route through existing NPCs over introducing strangers.
- Rotate your focus. Advance Kael/Mara, Elara/Silverwood, tax collector, and wolf
  bounty as SEPARATE plot threads. Do not dwell on one for more than two ticks in
  a row — pick up a different thread instead.
- The Silverwood is ONE possible source of trouble among several (Mara's
  smuggling, the absent tax collector, the wolf bounty, Noah's sealed letter).
  Do not over-rely on the Silverwood.
- Never contradict a standing tension — build on it.
